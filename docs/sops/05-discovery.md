## 1. Overview

Discovery is the litigation phase where both sides exchange information and documents. When the Defendant serves discovery on us, we have **30 days from the certificate of service date** to respond. When we serve discovery on the Defendant, they have the same obligation.

âš ï¸ **For RFAs:** If we don't answer within the time frame, all requests are **considered admitted by default.**

---

## 2. Types of Discovery

| Type | Abbreviation | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Request for Admissions | RFA | Defendant asks us to admit or deny specific statements |
| Interrogatories | INT | Defendant asks specific questions about the case |
| Request for Production of Documents | RFPD | Defendant requests specific documents |

## 3. Receiving Discovery â€” Immediate Steps

When discovery is received:

1. **Calendar the deadline:** 30 days from the date on the certificate of service
   - Add a **reminder 10 days before** the deadline, sent to Bart
2. **Request a Word document** from Opposing Counsel (OC) if discovery was not received in an editable format
3. **Send discovery to the client** via email:
   - Explain what discovery is
   - Advise them we need their help answering within **7 days**
4. **Call the client** to explain and provide clarification on what information is needed
   - Guide them on how to locate and provide requested documents
   - Document all communications in the CMS Activity section
5. **Standardize document formatting** while waiting for client responses (see [Section 10](#10-formatting-standards))
6. **Begin answering** routine/common questions with information available in the case file

## 4. Answering Requests for Admissions (RFAs)

### General Strategy

âš ï¸ **Try to deny everything**, except when you absolutely must admit.

- We usually admit to plaintiffs signing contracts with construction defect clients
- Insurance companies generally ask the same questions across cases

### Common RFA Questions (Insurance Cases)

> REQUEST NO. 1: Please admit or deny that the amount in controversy for the claims of the Plaintiffs for breach of contract as alleged in Count I of the Complaint does not exceed $75,000, exclusive of interest and costs.

> REQUEST NO. 2: Please admit or deny that the amount in controversy for the claims of the Plaintiffs against [Carrier] for violation of the Kentucky Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act, KRS 304.12-230 as alleged in Count II of the Complaint does not exceed $75,000, exclusive of interest and costs.

> REQUEST NO. 3: Please admit or deny that the aggregate of the claims contained in the Plaintiffs' Complaint against [Carrier], including but not limited to the claim for breach of contract in Count I and for violation of KRS 304.12-230 in Count II, do not exceed $75,000, inclusive of statutory interest and attorney fees but exclusive of non-statutory interest and attorney fees.

These are admitted or denied **depending on the case.**

### Objection Templates

You can object to an RFA if it is an impermissible compound question that calls for a legal conclusion:

> **REQUEST FOR ADMISSION NO. 3:** Admit or deny that Defendants did not cause any termite damage to your residence.
>
> **ANSWER:** Objection, impermissible compound question and calls for legal conclusion that there was termite damage to the Property. Denied.

### Tips
- Review other RFA answers from similar cases for reference
- If Bart works with OC who has multiple cases with us, they often ask the same questions

## 5. Answering Interrogatories

1. Carefully review all interrogatory questions
2. Review **ALL documents** in the case file to find relevant information
3. Answer common/routine questions first with readily available information
4. For the client's personal information (name, address, etc.):

> **ANSWER:** [Client Name] C/O Denham Property and Injury Law Firm.

5. Identify questions where information is incomplete â€” clearly outline what's needed
6. Submit to Bart for review before serving

---

## 6. Answering Requests for Production of Documents (RFPD)

For requests we are **not objecting to**, use this standard response:

> Plaintiff is producing all documents responsive to this request within his possession, custody, or control.

For requests we **are objecting to**, state all objections with legal grounds and privilege assertions.

---

## 7. Serving Our Discovery Requests

When we serve discovery on the Defendant:
1. Prepare Interrogatories and RFPD
2. Get attorney approval
3. Serve on OC
4. Calendar **30 days** for their response deadline

---

## 8. Following Up on Defendant Responses

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| 30 days from service | Check if defendant has responded |
| If no response | Send a letter of reminder |
| 30+ days late | File a **Motion to Compel** |

## 10. Formatting Standards

When formatting discovery responses:

- **Font:** Times New Roman, 12pt
- **Requests:** CAPITALIZED and **Bold**
- **Answers:** **Bold** and underlined
- **Spacing:**
  - Requests: 1.5 line spacing
  - Answers: 1.0 line spacing

**Example:**

> **INTERROGATORY NO. 1:** State your full name, any other name you have had or been known by, address, age, residence, business address, and occupation. *(1.5 spacing)*
>
> **<u>ANSWER:</u>** [Client Name] C/O Denham Property and Injury Law Firm. *(1.0 spacing)*

### Serving Discovery Answers

Once all questions are answered and reviewed by Bart:
1. Verify completeness and accuracy of ALL responses
2. Ensure all objections and privilege assertions are properly stated
3. Serve to OC
