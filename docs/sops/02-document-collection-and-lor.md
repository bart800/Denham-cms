## 5. LOR Follow-Up

**â±ï¸ Set a task for 2 days after the LOR is sent.**

### Kentucky Claims Follow-Up

Reply to the original LOR email:

> Please acknowledge receipt and provide the requested information, failure to do so will be used as further evidence of bad faith.
>
> "Failing to acknowledge and act reasonably promptly upon communications with respect to claims arising under insurance policies" is a breach of KRS 304.12-230(2) Unfair Claims Settlement Practices.
>
> "Failing to promptly provide a reasonable explanation of the basis in the insurance policy in relation to the facts or applicable law for denial of a claim or for the offer of a compromise settlement;" is a breach of KRS 304.12-230(14) Unfair Claims Settlement Practices.

### Montana Claims Follow-Up

> Please acknowledge receipt and provide the requested information. Failure to do so may constitute a violation of MCA 33-18-201, Montana's Unfair Claim Settlement Practices Act, including the failure to acknowledge and act reasonably promptly upon communications with respect to claims arising under insurance policies.

### All Other States Follow-Up

> Following up on this. Please acknowledge receipt and provide the requested information.

âš ï¸ **WE DO NOT FOLLOW UP ON CONTRACTOR NEGLIGENCE LORs.**

Save anything the adjuster sends to the CMS and OneDrive backup.

## 10. Scanning & Filing Procedures

### 10.1 Scanning Physical Documents

1. Open the **Windows Scan** app
2. Select the appropriate source:
   - **Stack of documents** â†’ Feeder
   - **Single-sided document or check** â†’ Flatbed
3. Set File Type to **PDF**
4. If double-sided, enable **"Scan both sides"**
5. Save file to the corresponding client folder
6. Remove any staples before scanning
7. Ensure documents are in correct order
8. Click **Scan**

### 10.2 Processing Scanned Documents in Adobe

1. Open the PDF in Adobe Acrobat
2. Under "All tools" â†’ select **"Scan & OCR"** â†’ **Recognize Text** â†’ In this file
   - This makes the PDF searchable
3. If the file needs to be split (e.g., a stack from the insurance company containing policy, estimates, etc.):
   - Go to **Edit** â†’ **Organize Pages**
   - Select the extract icon â†’ **"Extract pages as separate files"**
   - Rename files according to naming convention
   - Save to corresponding folders

### 10.3 Physical Filing

1. Ensure documents are in correct order
2. Re-staple if necessary
3. Place each document type in a labeled manila folder
4. Place in the corresponding hanging folder in the file cabinet
5. If no hanging folder exists, create one: **Last Name, First Name** (alphabetical order)

## 11. Common Mistakes & Pro Tips

### âš ï¸ Common Mistakes

1. **Sending LOR to the wrong email** â€” Double-check the adjuster email. Sending to a generic inbox may delay acknowledgment.
2. **Not including all insureds on the LOR** â€” If two people are on the policy, both names must be on the LOR. The CMS often only auto-populates one.
3. **Using the KY template for a non-KY case** â€” The KRS 304.14-100 paragraph only applies in Kentucky. Non-KY cases should use the non-KY template.
4. **Putting extra text in the subject line** â€” Subject line = claim number ONLY. Carriers use automated systems that parse subject lines.
5. **Forgetting to CC Bart and the CMS project email** â€” If it's not CC'd to the CMS, it doesn't get logged.
6. **Not following up on LOR within 2 days** â€” Carriers ignore LORs constantly. The follow-up is how we build the bad faith timeline.
7. **Not scanning mail immediately** â€” Physical mail sits in the inbox and gets lost. Scan the same day it arrives.

### ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

1. **For State Farm fire claims**, use `statefarmfireclaims@statefarm.com` if you don't have the adjuster's email. This actually works better than trying to find the direct email.
2. **When the adjuster name is unknown**, call the carrier's main claims line. Say: "I represent [client name], claim number [X]. I need the assigned adjuster's name and email." They'll give it to you 95% of the time.
3. **Save the adjuster's email as a contact** â€” You'll email them repeatedly throughout the case.
4. **When a PA refers a client**, ask the PA for the claim file immediately. They usually have the adjuster info, estimates, photos, and correspondence already.
5. **Set a recurring 1-week task** to follow up on LORs until the carrier responds with documents. Some carriers take 3-4 follow-ups.
