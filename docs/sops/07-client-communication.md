## 1. Overview

**Client satisfaction and concierge-level service are our top priorities.**

Every client interaction reflects on the firm. Timely communication builds trust and prevents complaints. Use the templates and procedures below to ensure consistent, professional updates.

## 3. Update Ownership

- The **primary attorney** or **assigned support staff** on the case is responsible for client updates
- After updating the client or responding to a request, **reply all** to the answering service email to confirm the update was handled
- The Office Manager keeps the answering service email distribution list up to date

---

## 4. What NOT to Tell Clients

âš ï¸ **Err on the side of caution.**

- Don't share information you're unsure about
- If you don't know the answer, tell the client you'll check with the team and get back to them
- **Always** follow through by the time you promised
- Don't speculate on case outcomes, timelines, or settlement amounts unless the attorney has provided specific guidance

---

## 5. What NOT to Tell Insurance Company Defendants

âš ï¸ **Be ESPECIALLY cautious in what you say.**

- Don't share any case information you're unsure about
- Take a message and either:
  - Ensure someone else reaches out to them, OR
  - Ask the attorney what to tell them exactly, then get back to them
- Never volunteer information â€” only respond to specific questions after getting guidance
