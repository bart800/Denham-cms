## 1. Overview

The settlement process covers everything from accepting a settlement offer through distributing funds to the client. This is one of the most critical SOPs ‚Äî errors here can result in trust account violations, bar complaints, and malpractice exposure.

**Full process flow:**

```
Settlement Offer Accepted ‚Üí Receive Settlement Docs ‚Üí Contact Client ‚Üí
Get Signatures/Notarization ‚Üí Deposit Check into IOLTA ‚Üí Wait 10 Business Days ‚Üí
Calculate Distribution ‚Üí Get Client Approval ‚Üí Distribute Funds ‚Üí Close File
```

**‚è±Ô∏è Time Expectations:**

| Task | Expected Time |
|------|--------------|
| Process settlement packet (scan, split, rename, save) | 30‚Äì45 min |
| Contact client and coordinate signatures | 15‚Äì30 min per attempt |
| Prepare distribution calculation | 20‚Äì30 min |
| Print and mail checks | 15‚Äì20 min |
| Full cycle (offer to distribution) | 2‚Äì4 weeks |

## 2. Settlement Offer Process

When presenting a settlement offer to Opposing Counsel:

1. **Get authorization to settle** from the client ‚Äî document the date, time, and amount authorized
2. **Attach the firm's W9** to the settlement communication
3. **Request check be issued to:**

### Preferred Format (Single Check)

> **Bart Denham Law PLLC F.B.O. [Insured Name(s)], [PA Name if applicable], [Mortgage Company if applicable]**

### Alternative Format (Split Check)

> ¬Ω to **Bart Denham Law PLLC F.B.O. [Insured Name(s)], [PA Name]**  
> ¬Ω to **the Mortgage Company and Client**

4. **Confirm with the client** that they have the same mortgage company on file ‚Äî mortgage companies change, and the wrong payee creates major problems

‚Üí See [SOP 03 ‚Äî Demand Drafting](03-demand-drafting-and-service.md) for demand letter procedures that precede settlement offers.

**‚è±Ô∏è Expected Time: 30‚Äì45 minutes**

2. Download the settlement packet
3. Open in Adobe Acrobat:
   - **All tools** ‚Üí **Scan & OCR** ‚Üí **Recognize Text** ‚Üí In this file (makes PDF searchable)
   - **Edit** ‚Üí **Organize Pages** ‚Üí Extract pages as separate files

   - ‚úÖ Settlement Letter
   - ‚úÖ Check
   - ‚úÖ Agreed Order of Dismissal (AOD)
   - ‚úÖ Settlement Agreement and Release
5. Rename files descriptively and save to the Settlement folder
6. **Log the settlement amount in the CMS** immediately

## 9. Settlement Check Handling & IOLTA Procedures

### 9.1 Receiving the Check

**‚è±Ô∏è Expected Time: 10‚Äì15 minutes**

1. **Scan** front and back of the settlement check immediately upon receipt
2. **Save** scanned image to:
   - CMS ‚Üí Negotiations section
   - OneDrive backup folder
   - **Naming format:** `[Date of check] - [Payor] - [Amount] - [Client Name]`
3. **Deliver** physical check to Office Manager
4. Office Manager logs the check details in the tracking spreadsheet

### 9.2 Endorsement Rules

| Check Says | Action Required |
|-----------|----------------|
| Written **FBO** | No POA needed ‚Äî endorsement by client only |
| Does NOT say FBO | POA needed, OR client must come in to endorse |
| Multiple parties listed | **All parties must sign** |
| Mortgage company listed | Mortgage company endorsement required (see [Section 14](#14-mortgage-company-procedures)) |

### 9.3 IOLTA Deposit Procedures

1. **Bart deposits** checks at the bank
2. Office Manager notes the **exact date of deposit**
3. ‚ö†Ô∏è **Clients must wait 10 business days after deposit** before any funds are disbursed
   - Count business days only (exclude weekends and bank holidays)
   - Use the date calculator: https://www.timeanddate.com/date/dateadd.html
4. During the waiting period, staff should prepare the distribution calculations
5. **Never disburse funds before the 10 business day hold clears** ‚Äî this is an ethical and regulatory requirement

### 9.4 IOLTA Record-Keeping

- Log every deposit with: date, amount, client name, source
- Log every disbursement with: date, amount, payee, purpose
- IOLTA account must balance at all times
- Monthly reconciliation required

## 10. Distribution Calculation Formula

### The Formula

```
Total Recovery = Undisputed Payment(s) + Settlement Amount

Attorney Fees = Total Recovery √ó Fee Percentage (from contract)

Net to Client = Total Recovery - Attorney Fees - Costs/Expenses - PA Fee
```

### Fee Percentage Decision

| Scenario | Fee % |
|----------|-------|
| Pre-suit settlement (no complaint filed) | Pre-litigation percentage (per contract) |
| Post-filing settlement (complaint filed) | Litigation percentage (per contract) |

‚ö†Ô∏è **Look in the client's file to determine if a complaint was filed** ‚Äî this determines which fee percentage applies.

## 11. Distribution Statement Preparation

**‚è±Ô∏è Expected Time: 20‚Äì30 minutes**

### 11.1 Create Distribution Spreadsheet

Include all of the following:

- **Total settlement amount** (include undisputed if applicable)
- **Attorney's fees** (per contract percentage ‚Äî verify pre-suit vs litigation)
- **Costs / Expenses:**
  - Filing fee (always if complaint filed, usually ~$300)
  - Mailing fees
  - Expert fees
  - Mediator fees (PI cases)
  - Deposition costs (PI cases)
- **Public Adjuster fees** (check PA contract ‚Äî **cannot exceed 10%**)
  - ‚ö†Ô∏è PA is the only other party we distribute fees to besides the client (in property cases)
- **Liens** (PI cases ‚Äî medical liens, health insurance subrogation, PIP subrogation)
- **Net to client**

### 11.2 Review Process

1. Email the distribution spreadsheet to **Bart for review**
2. Once approved, email the settlement breakdown to the client
3. Ask for POA if needed
4. Request a Google review

### 11.3 Log in CMS

- Go to the project ‚Üí **Expenses section** ‚Üí **New Award**
- Enter all settlement data (amount, fees, costs, net)

## 14. Mortgage Company Procedures

### When Is a Mortgage Company Involved?

- Property insurance settlement checks where there is an active mortgage on the property
- The mortgage company is typically listed as a loss payee on the insurance policy
- They **must** endorse the settlement check if their name is on it

### Procedure

1. **At settlement offer stage:** Confirm with client their current mortgage company (it may have changed since the policy was issued)
2. **When check arrives:** Check if mortgage company is listed as a payee
3. **If mortgage company IS on the check:**
   - Contact the mortgage company's loss draft department
   - Provide: claim number, policy number, client name, settlement amount
   - Follow their endorsement procedures (may require mailing the check to them)
   - ‚ö†Ô∏è This can add **2‚Äì4 weeks** to the distribution timeline
4. **Alternative ‚Äî split checks:**
   - Request ¬Ω to the mortgage company and client, ¬Ω to Bart Denham Law PLLC FBO client/PA
   - This avoids the mortgage company endorsement delay on the firm's portion

### Decision Tree

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Active mortgage + property settlement | Must include mortgage company |
| Mortgage recently paid off | Get payoff confirmation letter, proceed without |
| Client refinanced (new lender) | Update payee info before requesting check |
| No mortgage | No mortgage company needed |

## 15. Client Won't Sign Release

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Client disagrees with settlement terms | Schedule a call with the attorney to discuss; document concerns |

‚ö†Ô∏è **Never pressure a client to sign a release.** If they have concerns, the attorney must address them.

## 19. Common Mistakes & Pro Tips

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Wrong payee on check** | Check must be reissued ‚Äî adds weeks of delay | Triple-check payee names before sending payment instructions to OC |
| **Forgetting the mortgage company** | Check can't be cashed; must be reissued | Always ask client about current mortgage at settlement offer stage |
| **Not confirming mailing address** | Check goes to old address; security risk | Call/email client to confirm address before mailing ANY check |
| **Disbursing before 10 business day hold** | Trust account violation; regulatory exposure | Use date calculator and calendar the earliest disbursement date |
| **Using wrong fee percentage** | Overcharging or undercharging client | Check if complaint was filed ‚Üí litigation % vs pre-suit % |
| **Forgetting PA fee** | PA doesn't get paid; damages referral relationship | Always check if PA is involved and review PA contract for fee % |
| **Not scanning check immediately** | If check is lost, no record exists | Scan front AND back the moment it arrives |
| **Sending release with firm signature block** | Ethical violation ‚Äî firm cannot sign releases | Review every release for signature blocks; remove any for the firm |

### üí° Pro Tips

- **Prepare distribution calculations during the 10 business day hold** ‚Äî don't waste the waiting period
- **Keep a settlement tracking spreadsheet** with dates for every step ‚Äî Bart and the team need visibility
- **For PI cases:** Request updated lien payoffs right when settlement is reached ‚Äî don't wait until check arrives
- **Calendar the 10 business day hold end date** so you're ready to distribute the moment it clears
- **Always send the distribution breakdown to Bart first** before sharing with the client
- **Double-check math** on every distribution statement ‚Äî errors erode client trust
- **For split checks:** This is often faster when a mortgage company is involved ‚Äî suggest it proactively
- **Save all executed documents** (release, AOD, authority to distribute) to both CMS and OneDrive backup
