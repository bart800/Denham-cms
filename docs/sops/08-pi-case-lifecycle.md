## 1. Overview

Personal injury (PI) cases follow a different lifecycle than property insurance cases. This SOP covers the full PI case flow from intake through settlement or trial preparation. PI is the firm's secondary practice area.

**Key Differences from Property Cases:**
- Medical records and treatment tracking are central
- Multiple claim types may overlap (BI, PIP, UIM)
- Liens must be identified, tracked, and negotiated
- Settlement distribution is more complex due to liens and subrogation

**Typical PI Case Timeline:**

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Intake + LORs | 1â€“2 weeks |
| Treatment (client treating) | 2â€“12 months |
| Records collection | 2â€“6 weeks after treatment complete |
| Demand preparation + negotiation | 1â€“3 months |
| Litigation (if needed) | 6â€“12 months |
| Settlement distribution | 2â€“4 weeks |

## 9. PIP Claim Procedures

### What Is PIP?

PIP (Personal Injury Protection) is **no-fault** coverage from the client's own auto insurance policy. In Kentucky, PIP covers:
- Medical expenses (up to policy limits, typically $10,000)
- Lost wages (up to policy limits)
- Prescriptions

### When to File PIP

- **Immediately** â€” PIP should be activated at the beginning of every KY auto accident case
- Send the PIP LOR (Section 4) with the Application for No-Fault Benefits

### PIP Deadlines (Kentucky)

| Deadline | Timeframe |
|----------|-----------|
| File PIP application | As soon as possible after accident |
| PIP benefits must be paid | Within 30 days of receipt of proof of claim |
| PIP statute of limitations | 2 years from date of accident OR 2 years from last PIP payment |

### PIP Management

1. **Request monthly PIP ledgers** from the carrier
2. **Track PIP balance** â€” know how much has been paid and how much remains
3. **Reserve $2,000 for lost wages** (per PIP LOR)
4. **Hold ER bill** if it exceeds $2,000 â€” preserve PIP for ongoing treatment
5. **Do NOT let the carrier pay subrogation/liens from PIP** without our direction
6. At settlement, **PIP subrogation** must be addressed in the distribution

## 10. UIM/UM Claim Procedures

### Definitions

| Coverage | What It Covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **UM (Uninsured Motorist)** | Defendant has NO insurance |
| **UIM (Underinsured Motorist)** | Defendant's policy limits are less than client's damages |

### When to Pursue UIM

- Client's damages exceed the at-fault party's policy limits
- Client has UIM coverage on their own policy
- UIM is the **difference** between client's UIM limits and defendant's BI limits

### UIM Procedure

1. **At case start:** Request dec pages from client's carrier (included in PIP LOR)
2. **Verify UIM coverage exists** â€” check for signed rejection forms
3. **Request UIM adjuster assignment** (included in PIP LOR)
4. **After BI settlement:** Notify UIM carrier of the BI settlement amount
5. **Present UIM demand** showing total damages exceed BI recovery
6. **Negotiate UIM claim** separately

âš ï¸ **Important:** Some states require the UIM carrier to consent before settling the BI claim. Check state-specific rules.

â†’ See [SOP 09 â€” Calendaring & Deadlines](09-calendaring-and-deadlines.md) for UIM filing deadlines by state.

## 14. Lien Negotiation Procedures

### Why Negotiate Liens?

Medical liens and health insurance subrogation can significantly reduce the client's net recovery. Negotiating liens down increases the client's take-home.

### Types of Liens

| Lien Type | Typical Negotiation | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| **Health insurance subrogation** | 30â€“50% reduction | Governed by plan terms (ERISA vs state law) |
| **Hospital liens** | 20â€“40% reduction | Must be perfected to be valid |
| **Medicare/Medicaid** | Limited â€” federal rules apply | Must use Medicare Secondary Payer process |
| **Medical provider liens** | 25â€“50% reduction | Often willing to negotiate |
| **PIP subrogation** | Usually full amount | But confirm with carrier |

### Lien Negotiation Procedure

1. **Request updated lien amounts** from all lienholders (in writing)
2. **Calculate the client's net** after fees, costs, and full liens
3. **If net to client is unreasonably low**, negotiate:
   - Send a letter explaining: total recovery, attorney fees, costs, and what client would net
   - Ask the lienholder to reduce their claim proportionally
   - Common argument: "If we hadn't pursued this case, you would have recovered nothing"
4. **Get any lien reduction in writing** before distributing
5. **Document all negotiations** in CMS

### When to Negotiate

- **Before settlement:** Know your lien exposure so you can negotiate settlement amount accordingly
- **After settlement:** Negotiate liens down to maximize client's net recovery
- **Always get final payoff amounts in writing** with payment instructions
