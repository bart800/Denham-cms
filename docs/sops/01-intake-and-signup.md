## 1. Overview

The intake process is the first point of contact for potential new clients (PNCs). Whether the call is incoming (client found us via Google or referral) or outgoing (we received client info from a referral source), the goal is to:

1. Determine if the case is one we can take
2. Gather minimum required information
3. Send the Authority to Represent (ATR) contract
4. Request supporting documents
5. Set up the file in the CMS

âš ï¸ **First impressions go a long way!** Always act in a professional, friendly, and helpful manner.

## 3. Information Gathering

### 3.1 Create or Find the Lead
- **Cold call:** Create a new lead in the CMS
- **Answering service / website form:** Search by name first â€” the CMS usually auto-creates these

### 3.2 Capture Required Information

**Case Type:** Note whether MVA or Property Casualty in the CMS. Add key facts in the appropriate fields; add extra context in Notes.

**Date of Loss (DoL)** â€” Ask ASAP
- Required for the contract and for case viability
- âš ï¸ **If DoL > 1 year:** Request a FULL COPY OF THEIR POLICY (not just the declaration page). Under "Suit Against Us," the policy states the filing deadline.

**Case-Type Specifics:**
- **MVA:** Record accident location, injuries, whether still treating â€” enter in designated fields
- **Property Casualty:** Record insured property address and type of loss

### 3.3 Contacts & Identifiers
- Collect first/last name, property address, phone, email
- If someone else is on the policy: Add as a related contact (need name, phone/email, relationship)
- If available, get claim #, policy #, and adjuster contact info

### 8.1 Convert the Lead
1. Change lead status to **"Signed Up"**
2. Post the lead to the CMS case management system
3. Change the project name to: **Last Name, First Name v. Defendant(s)**

### Claim Over 1 Year Old

**Scenario:** Caller from Kentucky. Damage occurred more than 1 year ago.

**Key Explanation:**
> IS: Because your loss happened more than a year ago, Kentucky law and your insurance policy may limit the time to file suit. **If client doesn't have the full policy:**

## 13. Public Adjuster Referral Guide

### When to Send Us a Lead
- **Minimum claim value:** ~$20,000
- Good case indicators: SOL has not passed, claim over $20K or current client of the PA

### Preferred Intake Method
- **Most common:** Soft handoff â€” PA introduces us on an email chain, client confirms they're ready
- Clients may also schedule via Calendly, call, text, or email

### Documentation Before the Call (with client's permission)
- **Minimum:** Name, Date of Loss, Location, Short Summary
- **Nice to have:** Full policy, estimates, insurance correspondence, claim/policy numbers, photos

### Fee Schedule for PA Referrals
| Scenario | Pre-Lit | Litigation |
|----------|---------|------------|
| PA's current client | 25% | 33% |
| Not PA's current client | 33% | 40% |

### If We Reject
- We explore every avenue and discuss with the PA before rejecting
- If the PNC is the PA's client, we refer back to them
- If not, we refer to the local bar association's referral service
