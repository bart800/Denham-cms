## 1. Overview

Drafting a demand letter is a pre-litigation task. With it, we:
- Alert the insurance carrier that the claim exists and the claimant is prepared to pursue legal action
- Lay the foundation by providing key facts, policy provisions, and establishing liability and causation
- Support our position with evidence (photos, estimates, reports)
- Clearly state the itemized dollar amount and set a deadline for compliance

**Workflow:** Paralegal Draft â†’ Attorney Review â†’ Redraft as needed â†’ Attorney Review â†’ Paralegal Final Review â†’ SEND

âš ï¸ **Demand first draft should be completed within 5 days of ATR execution.** If delayed, notify Bart.

## 4. Proof of Loss (POL) Preparation

**â±ï¸ Expected Time: 30 minutes**

The Sworn Proof of Loss is drafted and sent to the client for signature **while the demand is being drafted**. Signatures must be completed before the demand is sent for review.

### 4.1 Requirements
- Policy with Declarations Page (impossible to complete without it)
- All estimates (to calculate the whole loss and damage value)

### 4.2 Drafting the POL

Use the firm's Sworn POL template (Adobe form). Fill in:

| Field | Where to Find | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Policy Number** | Declarations page | `000930969788` |
| **Company Claim Number** | Correspondence or client | `0709368203` |
| **Policy Amt. at Time of Loss** | "Limits of Liability" on dec page | `$250,000` |
| **Date Issued / Date Expires** | Policy Period on dec page | `01/15/2024 - 01/15/2025` |
| **Agent** | Listed on dec page as "Agent" (NOT the adjuster) | `John Smith, Allstate Agent` |
| **Agency Address** | Listed with agent name; leave blank if not available | â€” |
| **Insurance Company Full Name** | Policy â†’ verify on Dept. of Insurance | `Allstate Indemnity Company` |
| **Insurance Company Address** | Policy; if unavailable, Statutory Home Office | `PO Box 672041, Dallas, TX 75265` |
| **Insured Name(s)** | Check policy â€” include both if both are listed | `George D. Bonilla and Tammy R. Bonilla` |
| **Address of Insured** | As listed on the policy | `102 Frankie Dr., Richmond, KY 40475` |
| **Against loss by** | Cause of loss | `Wind/Hail` |
| **Date of Loss** | Verify against correspondence | `March 3, 2023` |
| **Occupancy** | Usually "Residential" | `Residential` |
| **Mortgage Company** | Listed on policy â€” include in Title and Changes | `US Bank` |
| **Loss & Amt. Claimed** | Whole loss BEFORE deductions | `$25,480.29` |

### 4.3 POL Tips

âš ï¸ **Do NOT subtract the deductible or prior payments** from the total amount claimed. The POL states the WHOLE loss value.

âš ï¸ **Verify the correct insurance company.** Some companies handle claims on behalf of others:
- **Sedgwick** handles claims for other insurers â€” check the certified policy
- **Liberty Mutual** handles claims for Wolverine Mutual
- Don't assume from correspondence â€” always verify on the policy

âš ï¸ If claiming losses under **multiple coverages** (e.g., dwelling + barn), add Coverage A + Coverage B limits together for the total policy amount.

âš ï¸ If there was a **policy change increasing coverage** during a relevant period, use the original one-year period but the **increased** coverage amount.

## 7. Settlement Breakdown Calculation

The breakdown consists of (if applicable):

| Item | Description |
|------|------------|
| **Estimate(s) for repairs** | May have multiple estimates |
| **Prior payments** | Amount already paid by carrier (subtract) |
| **Deductible** | Found in the policy (subtract) |
| **Contents** | Usually in fire claims; any claim with lost personal items |
| **Additional Living Expenses (ALE)** | If client displaced from home |
| **Attorneys' Fees, Costs, Interest, Bad Faith Release** | Added after Bart's review |

### Formula
```
Total = Estimates - Deductible - Prior Payments + ALE + Contents + [Attorneys' Fees if applicable]
```

## 16. Common Mistakes & Pro Tips

### âš ï¸ Common Mistakes

1. **Wrong insurance company name** â€” "State Farm" â‰  "State Farm Fire and Casualty Company." Always verify on the policy AND the Dept. of Insurance website.
2. **Dollar amounts don't match** â€” The narrative says $25,480 but the breakdown says $25,408. Triple-check.
3. **Wrong statute for the state** â€” Using KRS in an Indiana demand. Copy from the correct section above.
4. **Forgetting to subtract prior payments** â€” If Allstate already paid $7,644.06, your demand should net that out.
5. **Forgetting the deductible** â€” Or subtracting it when it should be included (varies by demand strategy â€” ask Bart).
6. **Misdated loss or policy period** â€” Verify against MULTIPLE sources (correspondence, policy, client).
7. **Missing or mislabeled enclosures** â€” If the demand references "the attached estimate" but the enclosures list doesn't include it, you'll look careless.
8. **Using "they" for the insurance company** â€” Insurance companies are "it." Always.
9. **Not updating the date when drafting spans multiple days** â€” If you started Monday and finish Wednesday, the date and deadline must reflect Wednesday.
10. **Sending the demand with Word comments still visible** â€” Remove ALL comments before converting to PDF.

### ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

1. **Read 2-3 completed demands from the Demands folder** before writing your first one. The best teacher is seeing what Bart approved.
2. **For roof damage, always cite 806 KAR 12:095 Â§ 9(1)(b)** â€” the matching requirement. Carriers hate this regulation because it usually means a full roof replacement.
3. **Embed photos** â€” A picture of hail damage with punctured ridge caps is worth 1,000 words of argument.
4. **Frame water losses carefully** â€” "Sudden water loss from a pipe bursting" is covered. "Slow leak over time" is generally NOT. Word choice matters.
5. **Use the denial letter against them** â€” If the carrier's own letter says "we inspected and found no damage," but photos show clear damage, quote their letter and show the contradiction.
