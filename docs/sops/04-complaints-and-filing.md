## 1. Overview

The Complaint is the document we file to proceed with litigation. Once filed, the case is in litigation. Insurance complaints use the firm's complaint template. Construction defect complaints do NOT have a separate template.

‚ö†Ô∏è The attorney must review the final Word version before filing. Allow **at least 48 hours** for review. Be mindful of out-of-office time and weekends.

## 3. Defendant Name Verification

‚ö†Ô∏è **You must include the FULL legal name of the insurance company.**

| Wrong ‚ùå | Right ‚úÖ |
|----------|---------|
| State Farm | State Farm Fire and Casualty Company |
| Allstate | Allstate Indemnity Company |
| Progressive | Progressive Home Insurance Company |
| USAA | USAA Casualty Insurance Company |

2. Click **"Insurers"** ‚Üí **"Insurer Search"**
3. Search by company name
4. Confirm the exact legal name

## 6. Drafting the Complaint

### 6.1 Add Custom Fields in CMS

Enter all required fields so they auto-populate into the template:

- ‚úÖ Claim Number
- ‚úÖ County (search the loss address ‚Äî county shown in results)
- ‚úÖ Date of Loss
- ‚úÖ Defendant FULL NAME (from policy ‚Üí verify with Dept. of Insurance)
- ‚úÖ Defendant Mailing Address (from LOR)
- ‚úÖ Defendant City, State, Zip
- ‚úÖ Location of Loss
- ‚úÖ Policy Number
- ‚úÖ Registered Agent Mailing Address (from Dept. of Insurance)

### 6.2 Generate from Template

1. Go to the client's Documents section in the CMS
2. Create document from template
3. Choose the correct template (‚Üí see [Section 5](#5-complaint-template-selection))
4. Save a copy to the **Pleadings** folder

### 6.3 Section-by-Section Review

#### County
Where the loss occurred determines the county.

#### Division and Civil Action No.
Leave as-is ‚Äî we don't receive these until the case is processed.

#### Plaintiff Name
Must match what appears on the Policy and Demand.

#### Defendant Name
Always the insurance company. Bart will instruct if additional defendants needed.

#### Home vs. Foreign Corporation

**At-home corporation** (headquartered in the state):
> At all times relevant and material hereto, [Insurance Company], was an at-home corporation, registered with the State of [State], and engaged in the business of providing insurance policies to property owners in [County] County, [State].

**Foreign corporation** (headquartered elsewhere):
> At all times relevant and material hereto, [Insurance Company], was a foreign corporation engaged in the business of providing insurance policies to property owners in [County] County, [State].

Check the insurance company's address on the Dept. of Insurance website.

#### Remaining Sections ‚Äî Check:

| Section | Verify |
|---------|--------|
| **Introduction** | Plaintiff name, plurality ("Plaintiff" vs "Plaintiffs") |
| **Jurisdiction and Venue** | County name |
| **Parties** | Plaintiff name, address; Defendant name, home/foreign |
| **General Allegations** | County, Insurance name, Policy #, DOL (format: "July 2, 2024"), Claim #, denied/delayed/underpaid |
| **Count I ‚Äî Breach of Contract** | Plurality; "failed to pay **any of**" vs. "failed to pay **the majority of**" |
| **Count II ‚Äî Statutory Bad Faith** | Plurality throughout |
| **Count III ‚Äî Common Law Bad Faith** | Plurality; "partially delaying" vs. "partially denying"; "to delay" vs. "to deny" |
| **Closing / Damages** | Client name and insurance company name |
| **Jury Demand** | ‚ö†Ô∏è **"For a trial of this cause by a jury" MUST be present ‚Äî ALWAYS** |
| **Date and Signature** | Update the date |

### 6.4 Submit for Review
Send to Bart for review. Bring questions or uncertainties to his attention.

## 8. Filing the Complaint (KY E-Filing)

‚ö†Ô∏è Bart needs to watch the first few times you file complaints.

### Step 1: Log In
- Go to: https://kcoj.kycourts.net/kyecourts/Login
- Log in with the attorney's credentials
- Click the blue **eFiling** box ‚Üí **e-Filing** on the red ribbon ‚Üí **e-File** ‚Üí **New Case**

### Step 2: Case Information
| Field | Selection |
|-------|-----------|
| **Attorney** | Enter attorney's name |
| **Court** | Select correct county (e.g., Madison, Fayette) |
| **Case Type** | Circuit Civil Action ‚Üí Contract over $5,000 |
| **Jury Demand** | Yes |
| **Case Type** | Contract - Other |

### Step 3: Party Information
- **Plaintiff:** Person ‚Üí enter client's name ‚Üí ‚ö†Ô∏è **DO NOT enter address**
- **Defendant:** Organization or Person ‚Üí enter name and address
- **Service Method:**
  - Person ‚Üí Certified Mail
  - Business ‚Üí Registered Agent of Service
- For multiple defendants: Click "Add Another Defendant" and repeat

### Step 4: Attorney & Additional Party Info
- Review attorney info ‚Üí Next
- Review RAOS info ‚Üí Next

### Step 5: Attach Documents
- ‚úÖ Attach Complaint (PDF)
- ‚úÖ Attach Exhibits (if applicable)
- ‚úÖ Attach Interrogatories and Requests for Production (PDF)
- Leave Motion, Additional Document, Proposed/Tendered Order, and Agreed Order blank

### Step 6: Final Review & Payment
- Review all information ‚Üí Next ‚Üí Payment Page
- Check confirmation box ‚Üí Continue to Payment ‚Üí Pay Now
- Ask Office Manager for credit card info if needed (by 4:30 PM the day before if possible)

### Step 7: Confirm Filing
- Check email for **Notice of Electronic Filing (NEF)**
- Wait for **Notice of Court Processing (NCP)**
- Save NCP to the CMS and OneDrive backup

| Deadline | Duration | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|

## 13. Common Mistakes & Pro Tips

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

1. **Wrong defendant name** ‚Äî "State Farm" instead of "State Farm Fire and Casualty Company." Verify on DOI website EVERY TIME.
2. **Entering plaintiff's address in the e-filing system** ‚Äî Do NOT enter the plaintiff's address. Only enter name.
3. **Forgetting to check plurality** ‚Äî If there are two plaintiffs, every "Plaintiff" must be "Plaintiffs" throughout the entire complaint.
4. **"Jury demand" missing** ‚Äî Must ALWAYS be present. If it's missing, we've waived our right to a jury trial.
5. **Wrong county** ‚Äî The county where the LOSS occurred, not where the client lives (unless they're the same).
6. **Filing without attorney review** ‚Äî Never file without Bart's written approval. Allow 48 hours.
7. **Home vs. foreign corporation wrong** ‚Äî Check DOI. If the company is headquartered in KY, it's "at-home." Otherwise, "foreign."
8. **Forgetting to serve discovery WITH the complaint** ‚Äî We almost always serve interrogatories and RFPD at the same time as filing. If you forget, the 45-day discovery clock doesn't start.
9. **Not saving the NCP** ‚Äî The Notice of Court Processing is proof of filing. Save it immediately.

### üí° Pro Tips

1. **Prepare the complaint and discovery at the same time** ‚Äî Bart reviews both together, and they're filed together.
2. **Request the credit card info the day BEFORE you plan to file** ‚Äî Asking at 4:35 PM when you need to file = bad day.
3. **Double-check "delaying" vs. "denying"** in Count III ‚Äî These words have different legal meanings. Use the correct one based on what the carrier actually did.
4. **Keep a screenshot of the e-filing confirmation page** ‚Äî In case the email confirmation is delayed or lost.
5. **For multiple-defendant cases**, confirm with Bart which defendants need to be served via RAOS vs. certified mail.
